title: Stress, arousal and calming down
source: general wellbeing guidance (original summary)

Short-term stress shifts the balance of the nervous system toward arousal, which can raise pulse and lower variability for a while. This is normal and passes. Simple calming practices — slow breathing with a longer exhale, a short walk, a pause away from screens — can help the body settle. If signs of elevated arousal are seen alongside a stressful period, a gentle suggestion to take a calming moment is appropriate. It is supportive wellbeing guidance only.
