title: Walking quality and steadiness
source: general wellbeing guidance (original summary)

Walking with a regular, even rhythm and good steadiness is part of everyday movement wellbeing. Cadence (steps per minute) and stride regularity naturally vary with terrain, footwear, tiredness, and mood. A one-off less-steady walk is usually nothing to read into. What is worth noticing gently is a persistent change from your own usual pattern — that can be a cue to rest, pay attention to footwear and surroundings, and, if it continues, to mention it to a professional.
