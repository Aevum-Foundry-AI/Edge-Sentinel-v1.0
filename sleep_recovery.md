title: Heart-rate variability and recovery
source: general wellbeing guidance (original summary)

Heart-rate variability (HRV) is the small beat-to-beat variation in your pulse. From a wrist optical sensor it is measured as pulse-rate variability, a close proxy. Higher values, relative to your own typical range, tend to go with a more rested, recovered state; lower-than-usual values often accompany fatigue, poor sleep, alcohol, or a hard previous day. HRV is very personal, so what matters is the trend against your own baseline, not comparison to anyone else. It is only reliable when you are still — movement adds noise. A gentle response to a lower reading is to prioritise rest, hydration, and an earlier night.
